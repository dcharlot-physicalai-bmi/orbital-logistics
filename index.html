Orbital logistics.

Physical AI for space logistics and transportation. Reaching a destination in space is a velocity budget, and the whole chain, from launch through orbital transfer, rendezvous, capture, and return, becomes tractable when reusable infrastructure and autonomous in-space transport pay it down. This is the instrument suite and the headless physics core behind it, so the capabilities can be tested, applied, and deployed toward an open, decentralized space economy.

The instruments
Δv architecture

Compose the journey

Stack reusable infrastructure and autonomous in-space transport against a destination's velocity budget. Exact rocket-equation mass ratios; payload read as a real mass fraction.

On-orbit capture

A chaser estimates a tumbling, non-cooperative target's pose with a Kalman filter, holds a certified approach corridor, matches the spin, and latches. Autonomous vs human-in-loop.

Open ↗ Momentum tether

Skyhook · rotovator

Real orbital mechanics and the constant-stress taper equation: catch and release velocities, the payload's transfer orbit, and the tether-to-payload mass by material.

Open ↗ EM launch

Maglev sled

Track length grows with the square of exit speed, capped by survivable g-load. Read the track, the peak power, and the coil-switching rate.

Open ↗ Active structure

Launch loop

An elevated track held up by a fast internal rotor: an unstable equilibrium stabilized span by span. Ordinary steel; the stiffness is the controller.
